Tumor predictions

The aim of this project is to work out the diagnosis by calculating the probability of various diseases given a set of radiological findings. Naturally Bay`s theorem could be used to calculate the probabilities. Baye`s theorem asserts that the probability of a disease can be calculated if we know the prevalence of the disease in the society and the conditional probability of the radiological sign given that disease. The conditional probability of the sign given the disease is simply the sensitivity of that sign to the disease. In other words, if we know the sensitivities of the different radiological signs in different diseases, we can calculate the probability of each disease given a particular set of radiological signs.

However, two potentials drawbacks may arise when strictly applying the above equation: Firstly, suppose one or more findings in our query case is not normally associated with a particular disease (literally the sensitivity of those findings=0) what will be the probability of that disease given those findings? Well, the nominator in the above equation becomes zero because at least one sensitivity of those findings will be equal to zero leading to a zero probability. But having a zero probability of a disease is not a generally desirable thing. So we require that the probability of all diseases can not be zero. Secondly, suppose on the other hand that our query case has no particular finding that happens to be so specific for a particular disease, wouldn't it intuitively mean that the odds of that disease is reduced when that specific variable does not exist in our query case? On the other hand, when the query case has no such variable that is specific to a particular disease the modulator number absentj will reduce the probability of that disease as desired by the second requirement.
